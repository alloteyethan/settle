🛠️ The Architecture of SETTLE
1. The Core Philosophy
The Seller App: A Progressive Web App (PWA) or minimalist mobile app. They sign up, link their mobile money (MoMo) or bank account, and manage deals.

The Buyer Interface: A clean, zero-login web landing page. They click a link, view details, pay via MoMo/Card, and walk away. Zero friction.

The Trust Anchor: The UI explicitly states that the funds are held securely in a regulated partner trust account (e.g., “Secured via [Partner Bank/MTN MoMo Escrow Wallet]”). You are the software layer; an established giant is the vault.

📱 Main Platform Features & UI Breakdown
Feature 1: The One-Tap Deal Generator (Seller Dashboard)
The primary screen for the seller is an ultra-clean creation tool. No cluttered inventory management. Just a cash register.

Inputs Needed:

Item Name / Description (e.g., "iPhone 13 Pro Max - Grade A")

Price (GHS)

Delivery Window (Default: 48 Hours)

The Magic Button: [ Generate Settle Link ]

The Output: A unique, short URL (settle.shop/x9B2f7) and an auto-generated text block ready to copy:

"Hey! Let's secure our deal safely using SETTLE. Your funds will be locked securely until you receive and verify the item. Click here to pay: settle.shop/x9B2f7"

Feature 2: The Zero-App Checkout (Buyer Web Portal)
When the buyer clicks the link inside WhatsApp, they see a highly polished, single-page application that looks like a clean receipt.

Screen Elements:

The Trust Banner: Clear badge showing the money is locked in a secure partner vault.

Deal Details: What they are buying and the exact price.

The Security Statement: "Your money will not be released to the seller until you confirm delivery or 48 hours pass with no complaints."

Payment Trigger: Single button for [ Pay & Lock Funds ] integrating local payment APIs (Hubtel, Paystack, or Flutterwave) to prompt an instant MoMo STK Push.

Feature 3: The 48-Hour Escrow Engine (The State Machine)
This is the backend logic that controls the life cycle of the transaction. A transaction must only ever have 5 states:

[1. Created] ➡️ [2. Locked / Paid] ➡️ [3. Dispatched] ➡️ [4. Delivered / Received] ➡️ [5. Settled]
State 1 (Created): Link exists, waiting for payment.

State 2 (Locked): Buyer pays. Seller gets a WhatsApp/SMS alert: "Payment locked! You can now safely dispatch the item."

State 3 (Dispatched): Seller taps "I have shipped/delivered the item." This triggers the 48-Hour Countdown to the buyer.

State 4 (Delivered): The buyer gets an automated text link: "Your item has been marked as delivered. Tap here to confirm receipt or report an issue."

State 5 (Settled): Either the buyer taps "Confirm" OR the 48 hours expire with no complaints. The backend instantly releases the funds to the seller's wallet.

⚠️ The Dispute & Complaint System (The Reality Check)
If a buyer taps "There is a Problem" during the 48-hour window, the transaction freezes immediately. To keep it simple and prevent platform abuse, the system behaves as follows:

Categorization: The buyer must select one of exactly three options:

Item Never Arrived

Wrong/Damaged Item

Incomplete Service

Evidence Collection: The buyer must upload a single piece of proof (a photo of the wrong item or a screenshot of delivery failure).

The Resolution Portal:

The seller is notified that funds are frozen.

The seller can either click [ Issue Full Refund ] to end it instantly, or [ Submit Counter-Proof ] (e.g., waybill receipt, rider tracking).

If contested, it escalates to your minimalist admin panel for a quick, manual 5-minute review based purely on the documents uploaded.

Before you write the first line of code, lock down these three operational decisions:
